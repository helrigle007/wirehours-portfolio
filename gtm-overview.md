# Go-to-Market Overview

A community-first approach to acquiring users in a trust-sensitive trade market.

---

## Market Characteristics

The electrical trade has unique dynamics that shaped the entire GTM strategy:

- **Tight-knit communities** — Apprentices, journeymen, and masters interact daily on forums like ElectricianTalk, Mike Holt Forum, and Reddit's r/electricians. Reputation matters.
- **Deep skepticism of outsiders** — Trade communities quickly identify and reject promotional content. "Are you selling something?" is the fastest way to lose credibility.
- **Word-of-mouth is king** — A recommendation from a fellow electrician carries 10x the weight of any ad.
- **High lifetime value** — An apprentice who starts tracking hours in year one will use the tool for 4+ years through their entire apprenticeship.

These characteristics made one thing clear: a credibility-first approach was the only viable path.

---

## Strategy: Earn Trust, Then Earn Revenue

### Phase 1 — Establish Expertise (Weeks 1–2)

The first two weeks involved zero product promotion. Instead:

- **Daily forum engagement** answering apprentice questions about hour requirements, state licensing processes, and documentation strategies
- **SEO content creation** — state-specific licensing guides targeting long-tail keywords like `[State] journeyman electrician requirements` and `electrical apprentice hour tracking`
- **Voice of Customer research** — documenting the exact language and emotional states of apprentices discussing hour tracking problems

The goal was to become a recognizable, helpful username in these communities before ever mentioning a product.

### Phase 2 — Soft Introduction (Weeks 3–4)

With credibility established, product mentions were introduced contextually:

- **Story-driven launch posts** framed as "I built this tool and I'm looking for feedback" — not sales pitches
- **Trade school and union chapter outreach** positioning WireHours as a free resource for their students/members
- **Targeted Google Ads** on high-intent keywords (`electrical apprentice hour tracker`, `track apprentice hours app`)
- **Content marketing expansion** addressing specific pain points surfaced during Phase 1 research

### Phase 3 — Scale & Optimize (Weeks 5–6)

- **Conversion funnel optimization** based on analytics (signup → onboarding → first hour logged → Pro upgrade)
- **Email nurture sequences** for free users, demonstrating Pro value through state-specific insights
- **Product Hunt and Hacker News launches** for broader visibility
- **Contractor-tier outreach** targeting electrical companies managing multiple apprentices (higher ARPU)

## Channel Strategy

### Organic / Community (Primary)

| Channel | Approach | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Reddit (r/electricians, r/IBEW) | Helpful answers → story-driven product posts | Large apprentice population, authentic engagement rewarded |
| ElectricianTalk | Pure value contribution (strict anti-promo policies) | High-trust community, industry veterans |
| Mike Holt Forum | Technical discussions, licensing guidance | Experienced audience, strong SEO authority |

### Content / SEO (Compounding)

- **State licensing guides** — one page per state, each targeting `[State] electrical apprentice requirements`
- **Blog posts** addressing forum-sourced pain points with genuine depth
- **Lead magnets** — printable hour log templates that demonstrate the paper tracking problem

Each piece of content serves dual purpose: SEO entry point and genuine value delivery.

### Paid (Supplementary)

- Google Ads on high-intent, low-competition keywords specific to apprentice hour tracking
- Budget allocated based on measured CPC and conversion data, not assumptions

### Outreach (B2B Pipeline)

- Trade schools and apprenticeship programs → free tool for students
- IBEW local chapters → resource for members
- Electrical contractors → Contractor tier for team management

## Key Learnings

**Authenticity is non-negotiable.** Even slightly promotional language triggers rejection in trade forums. The most effective posts are ones where the product mention feels like a natural part of solving someone's stated problem.

**SEO compounds.** State-specific guides continue driving traffic long after publication. Each new state page is a new entry point.

**The emotional hook matters more than features.** "Don't let 4 years of work turn into 6,500 documented hours" resonates more than "Track hours with state-specific categories."

**B2B has faster revenue dynamics.** A single contractor subscribing at $49/month + $8/user for 10 apprentices generates more revenue than 20 individual Pro subscriptions.
